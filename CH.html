The Sixth Section of the Bible

The book of Acts bridges the Gospels and the Epistles, recording the birth and early growth of the Christian Church through the power of the Holy Spirit.

The Acts of the Apostles

使徒行传 / 使徒行傳

The Birth of the Church

Author: Luke (Physician & Historian)
Written: Around AD 63
Chapters: 28

Key Events:

Jesus' ascension into Heaven, The Day of Pentecost and the coming of the Holy Spirit, The conversion of Saul (Paul), Peter's vision and ministry to the Gentiles, Paul's missionary journeys, The Jerusalem Council, Paul's trials and journey to Rome

About This Book:

Acts serves as a sequel to the Gospel of Luke, continuing the story of God's work through Jesus Christ by documenting how the early Church spread from Jerusalem to Rome. It chronicles the transition from a predominantly Jewish movement to a Church that welcomed Gentiles, showing how the Holy Spirit empowered ordinary people to become witnesses to the ends of the earth. The book focuses particularly on the ministries of Peter (chapters 1-12) and Paul (chapters 13-28), demonstrating the unstoppable growth of the Gospel message despite persecution and opposition.
