Books of Poetry

Job

约伯记 / 約伯記

Trusting in God's Wisdom in Times of Suffering

Author: Unknown
Written: Unknown (possibly 2000-1500 BC)
Chapters: 42

About This Book:

The story of Job explores profound questions about suffering, faith, and God's sovereignty. Job, a righteous man, faces tremendous trials and maintains his faith despite overwhelming loss and pain. The book addresses the age-old question: "Why do the righteous suffer?"

Psalms

诗篇 / 詩篇

Joy, Pain & Prayer

Authors: Multiple (mostly King David)
Written: Around 1500 BC - 930 BC
Chapters: 150

About This Book:

The Book of Psalms is a collection of songs, prayers, and poems expressing the full range of human emotion before God—from deepest despair to highest praise. These ancient hymns have been used in worship for thousands of years and speak to the heart of every believer.

Proverbs

箴言 / 箴言

The Book of Wisdom

Authors: Multiple (mostly King Solomon)
Written: Around 970 BC - 686 BC
Chapters: 31

About This Book:

Proverbs offers practical wisdom for daily living, covering topics like work, family, speech, and relationships. These short, memorable sayings teach us how to live skillfully and righteously, beginning with the principle that "the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom."

Ecclesiastes

传道书 / 傳道書

The Search for Meaning

Author: King Solomon (The Preacher)
Written: Around 935 BC
Chapters: 12

About This Book:

Ecclesiastes examines life's meaning and purpose, exploring the emptiness of worldly pursuits apart from God. The book's famous refrain "vanity of vanities" leads to the conclusion that true meaning is found only in fearing God and keeping His commandments.

Song of Songs

雅歌 / 雅歌

A Celebration of Love

Author: King Solomon
Written: Around 965 BC
Chapters: 8

About This Book:

Song of Songs (also called Song of Solomon) is a beautiful poetic celebration of romantic love and marriage. Many also see it as an allegory of God's love for His people and Christ's love for the Church, demonstrating that love is a gift from God.
